# Camp Ukai Community Agreement (Draft)

*Adapted and condensed from the Hex House Community Agreement, which was adapted from the School for Poetic Computation's Community Agreement (co-written by Zainab Aliyu, Melanie Hoff, Celine Katzman, and Galen Macdonald). Open for feedback and revision.*

## Why we have this

Camp Ukai is a shared space for gathering, creating, and celebrating together. Safety is relative and always being negotiated — and discomfort isn't the same as unsafety. There's no such thing as a space that's never uncomfortable. This agreement exists so everyone at Camp Ukai understands how we relate to each other, and what happens if that trust is broken.

## Who this applies to

Everyone at Camp Ukai — organizers, campers, guests, and visitors — agrees to uphold this agreement in all camp spaces, physical and online (group chats, socials, etc.). This covers how we treat each other, the feedback we give, what we make together, and how we show up in shared space.

This agreement belongs to everyone. People in positions of power are just as accountable to it as anyone else — and just as capable of making mistakes. This isn't about punishment; it's about making mistakes gracefully, unlearning harmful patterns, and moving toward repair together.

## Our values in practice

**Center those most at risk.** Prioritize the safety of BIPOC, queer, trans, disabled, femme, low-income, and other marginalized folks over the comfort of those in more privileged positions. Harassment, threats, offensive comments, and unwelcome sexual attention are never acceptable.

**Believe each other.** Actively listen to and honor others' lived experience, even when it differs from your own. Don't make marginalized people carry the emotional labor of educating others — that work belongs to those with privilege.

**Respect space and each other.** Ask how people want to be addressed rather than assuming. Be mindful of how much space you take up in conversation — if you talk a lot, practice listening; if you tend to hold back, practice speaking up. Silence is okay.

**Practice consent.** Ask before physical contact. Give a heads-up before sharing potentially triggering content. Keep personal disclosures inside the group unless you have permission to share them elsewhere. Credit others' work and ask before using it.

**Be mindful of power dynamics.** Organizers should not pursue new sexual or romantic relationships with campers.

**Stay open to discomfort.** Growth requires vulnerability and the willingness to make mistakes, admit them, and take feedback as a gift.

**Take accountability.** If someone tells you that you hurt them, thank them and apologize — focus on impact, not intent. Save the deeper reflection on intent for later, on your own or with a friend.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any concern, contact the camp organizers at **[EMAIL PLACEHOLDER]**. If the person involved is an organizer, reach out to a different one directly: **[NAMES/CONTACTS PLACEHOLDER]**.

We take all good-faith reports seriously, including reports about organizers and reports of behavior that happened outside camp or in the past. We'll keep things confidential when asked — though at our discretion, we may warn others or name someone publicly if we believe it's needed for community safety. We will never name a person who experienced harm without their consent.

## What happens next

Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behavior is expected to stop immediately. Organizers may take any action they judge appropriate, up to and including removal from camp spaces (in person and online) without warning. Re-entry to the community is possible if it's judged to support that person's growth — with the needs of whoever was harmed always coming first.
